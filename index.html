ARC GIS Map Showcase

New Philly Store

The Problem: Find an area for a new healthy food store in Philadelphia that is near a public transport station, far enough away from other healthy food stores and markets and is a large enough space. The Plan: I knew I needed to split the problem into 2 main groups, the areas where it couldnâ€™t be and the areas where it could. Then from there I could overlay the two and find all available spaces. For both sub groups it involved buffering the variables to map a distance around and create the usable spaces dissolved from that. When I had both inclusive and exclusive I could overlay them and using the erase tool I was able to dissolve the spaces that meet all the criteria then separate them into multiple shapes. To find the largest area I generated the area field for my valid shapes and determined the highest to lowest values. To create the layout for my map I used unique value symbology to show the areas for each color coded valid shape. The Solution: There were 5 areas that met the criteria and had the following areas in square feet: Red: 3,760 sq. ft., Purple: 17,431 sq. ft., Pink: 44,506 sq. ft., Blue: 1,090,003 sq. ft., Green: 4,049,487 sq. ft. I determined that the best area for the new store is the green space because it has the largest available area that fits all the criteria. What I learned in GIS: I learned how to use multiple new tools such as buffering, dissolving, and overlaying data to map specific areas that meet sets of criteria.
